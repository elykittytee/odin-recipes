Leche Flan

Description

Leche flan is a Filipino dessert steamed in a baking pan called a llanera. This recipe has several steps: caramelizing the sugar for the llanera "topping", preparing the flan, and then steaming the flan.

Ingredients

  • 12 Egg Yolks
  • 3 drops of Vanilla
  • 1 can of Evaporated Milk (12 fl oz)
  • 1 can of Condensed Milk (14 oz)
  • 1-2 tbsp of White Sugar

You will also need:

  • a large bowl
  • a small pitcher for pouring into the llanera
  • llanera
  • a steamer
  • a mixer (we use an immersion blender)
  • a sieve
  • a serving plate that is larger in diameter than the llanera

This recipe makes about 3 flans in a traditional llanera

Recipe Directions

Caramelize the Sugar in the Llanera

  1. Put about 1 tbsp of sugar in the llanera
    • The amount of sugar needed is dependent on the size of your llanera
    • When the sugar liquifies, the sugar should completely coat the bottom of the llanera and then a little some.
  2. Gently brown the liquified sugar until it's a golden caramel color.
  3. Don't burn the sugar
  4. Let sugar cool.

Prepare the Flan Mixture

  1. Place egg yolks, both cans of milk, and vanilla in a bowl and blend until smooth.
  2. Using a sieve, strain and pour the mixture into your pitcher
  3. Make sure the sugar in the llanera is cooled.
  4. Pour flan mixture into the llanera, leaving about a cm to a cm and a half of space between the top and the flan itself.
  5. Gently tap the bottom of the llanera, to force the bubbles to rise.

Steam

  1. Cover each llanera with foil.
  2. Fill bottom of the steamer vessel with water.
  3. Once the water is boiling, place the llanera in the pot.
  4. Cover the pot. Let cook for 30-35 minutes.
  5. Leche Flan is ready when the flan is jiggly but firm.

Serve the flan by confidently flipping it over onto its serving plate.
